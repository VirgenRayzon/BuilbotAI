# Motherboard Selection Guide

Selecting a motherboard involves balancing the Socket, Chipset, VRM quality, and Connectivity.

## 1. Choosing the Right Chipset

### Intel (LGA1700 / LGA1851)
*   **Z-Series (Z790/Z890):** High-end. Supports CPU overclocking, more PCIe lanes, and better VRMs. Best for i7/i9.
*   **B-Series (B760):** Mid-range. No CPU overclocking, but supports RAM overclocking (XMP). Best for i5/i7 non-K.
*   **H-Series (H610):** Entry-level. Limited features. Use only for budget i3/i5 office builds.

### AMD (AM4 / AM5)
*   **X-Series (X670/X870):** Enthusiast level. Maximum connectivity, dual-chipsets (on AM5), and best VRMs.
*   **B-Series (B650/B850):** The sweet spot. Supports PBO (Precision Boost Overdrive) and good connectivity.
*   **A-Series (A520/A620):** Budget. Locked settings. Avoid for high-TDP CPUs (Ryzen 9).

## 2. VRM Quality
The VRM (Voltage Regulator Module) converts power for your CPU. If the VRMs are weak, a high-power CPU (like an i9-14900K) will "thermal throttle" even if the CPU cooler is great, because the motherboard itself is overheating.
*   **Tip:** Look for motherboards with large, chunky metal heatsinks around the CPU socket.

## 3. Form Factors
*   **ATX:** Standard size. Most features and expansion slots.
*   **Micro-ATX (mATX):** Smaller, often cheaper. Fits in most cases.
*   **Mini-ITX (ITX):** For small form factor (SFF) builds. Very expensive and hard to build in.

## 4. Modern Features to Look For
*   **WiFi 6E / 7:** Essential if you aren't using Ethernet.
*   **M.2 Heatsinks:** Prevents fast SSDs from slowing down due to heat.
*   **BIOS Flashback:** Allows you to update the BIOS without a CPU installed (vital for new CPU compatibility).

---
> [!IMPORTANT]  
> Ensure your motherboard choice matches your case size! An ATX motherboard will NOT fit in a Micro-ATX or ITX case.
