# Community Recommended Hardware Resources

When building a PC, the enthusiast community (Reddit, LTT, etc.) relies on specific "Tier Lists" and authoritative sources to judge component quality beyond just marketing specs. Use these resources to ensure you're getting high-quality parts for your budget.

## 1. Power Supply (PSU) Tier List
**Source: Cultists Network**
- PSUs are categorized from **Tier A (Top Tier)** to **Tier E (Avoid)**.
- Always aim for Tier A or B for high-end builds. 
- Avoid any PSU listed in the "Tier E / Avoid" or "Tier F / Replace" categories, as they can represent a fire hazard or risk damaging other components.

## 2. SSD Buying Guide
**Authoritative Source: u/NewMaxx**
- SSD performance isn't just about "Sequential Speed." Factors like **DRAM Cache**, **Controller Type**, and **NAND Layers** determine long-term reliability and OS responsiveness.
- Use experts like NewMaxx to distinguish between "Budget" NVMe drives (fine for gaming) and "Pro" drives (needed for heavy video editing).

## 3. GPU & CPU Hierarchies
**Source: Tom's Hardware**
- The standard for comparing raw framerates across generations.
- Use these hierarchies to see if a current mid-range card (e.g., RTX 4070) beats a previous-gen flagship (e.g., RTX 3090).

## 4. Why Use These Lists?
- **Avoid Overpaying:** A "Gold Rated" PSU might still be low quality. The Tier List tells you the internal component quality.
- **Identify Bottlenecks:** Using a Tier List helps ensure your SSD and PSU are in the same performance "class" as your CPU and GPU.
- **Future Proofing:** Higher-tier components usually feature better warranties and longer lifespans.

---
*Note: Treat these lists as reference guides. Quality can change with new component revisions, so always check for the most recent version of the list.*
