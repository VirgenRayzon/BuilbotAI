# GPU Tier List (Q1 2026)

This document provides a general ranking of modern GPUs for gaming performance across different resolutions based on rasterization and ray tracing capabilities.

## Tier S (Uncompromised 4K / Enthusiast)
These GPUs offer the absolute maximum performance currently available, capable of pushing high framerates at 4K resolution with max settings and full ray tracing.

*   NVIDIA GeForce RTX 5090
*   NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4090
*   AMD Radeon RX 8900 XTX
*   AMD Radeon RX 7900 XTX

## Tier A (High-End 4K & Elite 1440p)
Excellent choices for 4K gaming with some settings adjustments, or blistering fast 1440p performance.

*   NVIDIA GeForce RTX 5080
*   NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4080 Super
*   NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4080
*   AMD Radeon RX 7900 XT
*   AMD Radeon RX 8800 XT

## Tier B (Solid 1440p / Entry 4K)
 The "sweet spot" for modern 1440p monitors, offering high framerates without breaking the bank entirely.

*   NVIDIA GeForce RTX 5070 Ti
*   NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4070 Ti Super
*   NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4070 Ti
*   NVIDIA GeForce RTX 5070
*   NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4070 Super
*   AMD Radeon RX 7900 GRE
*   AMD Radeon RX 7800 XT

## Tier C (Mainstream 1080p / Entry 1440p)
Great for players targeting a smooth 1080p experience or stepping into 1440p with modest expectations.

*   NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4070
*   NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4060 Ti (16GB & 8GB)
*   AMD Radeon RX 7700 XT
*   AMD Radeon RX 7600 XT
*   AMD Radeon RX 6750 XT

## Tier D (Budget / Entry-level 1080p)
Capable of 60FPS at 1080p on medium settings, ideal for casual or esports gaming.

*   NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4060
*   NVIDIA GeForce RTX 3060 (12GB)
*   AMD Radeon RX 7600
*   AMD Radeon RX 6600 XT
*   AMD Radeon RX 6600
*   Intel Arc A770

## General Advice
When pairing a GPU, ensure the Power Supply (PSU) has sufficient wattage (e.g., 850W+ for Tier S/A, 750W for Tier B). Avoid pairing a Tier S GPU with an older generation CPU (like a Ryzen 3000 or Intel 10th gen) to prevent severe CPU bottlenecks at 1440p and 1080p.
