# SSD Tier List & Performance Guide

SSDs are categorized by their interface (NVMe Gen5, Gen4, Gen3, or SATA) and the presence of a DRAM cache.

## Tier 1: Professional / Extreme (Gen4/Gen5)
*Top-tier performance for 4K video editing, heavy workstation tasks, and high-end gaming.*

*   **Samsung:** 990 Pro, 980 Pro.
*   **WD Black:** SN850X, SN850.
*   **Crucial:** T705 (Gen5), T700 (Gen5), P5 Plus.
*   **SK Hynix:** Platinum P41.
*   **Seagate:** FireCuda 530.

## Tier 2: Performance Gaming (Gen4)
*The "Sweet Spot" for modern gaming builds.*

*   **Samsung:** 980 (Non-Pro).
*   **WD Black:** SN770 (DRAM-less but very fast).
*   **Kingston:** KC3000, Fury Renegade.
*   **Crucial:** P3 Plus (Gen4, QLC - good for storage, okay for OS).

## Tier 3: Budget / Mainstream (Gen3 / SATA)
*Perfect for general use, secondary storage, or budget builds.*

*   **WD Blue:** SN580, SN570.
*   **Samsung:** 970 Evo Plus.
*   **Crucial:** P3 (Gen3).
*   **SATA Picks:** Samsung 870 Evo, Crucial MX500 (Best SATA drives with DRAM).

## What is DRAM Cache?
DRAM cache is a dedicated memory chip on the SSD that stores the data map. SSDs with DRAM are generally faster and last longer when used as an Operating System (C:) drive. DRAM-less drives (like the WD SN770) use "HMB" (Host Memory Buffer) to borrow your system RAM, which is almost as good but slightly slower.

---
> [!TIP]  
> For a purely gaming PC, you will rarely notice the difference between a Gen3 and Gen4 NVMe drive. Spend the extra money on capacity rather than speed if budget is tight.
