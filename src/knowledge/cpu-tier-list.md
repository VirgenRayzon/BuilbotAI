# CPU Tier List (2025 Edition)

This guide ranks CPUs based on gaming performance and multi-core productivity.

## Tier S (Extreme Performance)
*Best for high-refresh 4K gaming and heavy workstation tasks (Redline builds).*

*   **AMD:** Ryzen 9 9950X, Ryzen 9 7950X3D, Ryzen 7 9800X3D, Ryzen 7 7800X3D (Gaming King).
*   **Intel:** Core i9-14900KS, Core i9-14900K, Core Ultra 9 285K.

## Tier A (High-End)
*Excellent for 4K/1440p gaming and content creation.*

*   **AMD:** Ryzen 9 9900X, Ryzen 7 9700X, Ryzen 9 7900X3D, Ryzen 7 7700X.
*   **Intel:** Core i7-14700K, Core i7-13700K, Core Ultra 7 265K.

## Tier B (Mid-Range / Sweet Spot)
*Best value for 1440p gaming.*

*   **AMD:** Ryzen 5 9600X, Ryzen 7 7700 (Non-X), Ryzen 5 7600X, Ryzen 7 5800X3D (AM4 Legend).
*   **Intel:** Core i5-14600K, Core i5-13600K, Core Ultra 5 245K.

## Tier C (Mainstream / Budget Gaming)
*Great for 1080p gaming and general use.*

*   **AMD:** Ryzen 5 7600, Ryzen 5 7500F, Ryzen 7 5700X3D, Ryzen 5 5600.
*   **Intel:** Core i5-13400, Core i5-12400.

## Tier D (Entry Level)
*Basic gaming or office productivity.*

*   **AMD:** Ryzen 5 5500, Ryzen 3 4100.
*   **Intel:** Core i3-14100, Core i3-12100.

---
> [!IMPORTANT]  
> **X3D CPUs** (e.g., 7800X3D) are specifically optimized for gaming due to their massive L3 cache. They often outperform higher-core count CPUs in games, even if their productivity scores are lower.
